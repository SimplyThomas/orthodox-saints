## 3. What was built this phase

OS-0401–0500 is the densest monastic stretch so far — Cappadocian, Egyptian/Judean/Sinai desert,
Georgian, Serbian, and Russian Northern-Thebaid clusters all land here:

- **Cappadocian household** — OS-0420 turned out to be **Peter of Sebaste**, completing the family
  of St. Basil the Great (parents Basil the Elder OS-2757 & Emilia OS-0374, grandmother Macrina the
  Elder OS-2474, siblings Basil OS-0021 / Gregory of Nyssa OS-0422 / Macrina the Younger OS-1551 /
  Theosebia OS-0427). Now a fully cross-linked `household` (group candidate).
- **Judean Desert** — **Euthymius the Great** OS-0494 became a dense hub (Sabbas, Theoctistus,
  Theodosius the Cenobiarch, Gerasimus of the Jordan, Kyriakos, Juvenal, Chariton).
- **Sinai & Raithu** — the slain fathers (OS-0450/0451/0452) wired into the wider Sinai tradition
  (John Climacus, Anastasius, Nilus).
- **Georgia** — the **Thirteen Syrian Fathers** (Anthony of Martqopi OS-0493 + David of Gareji and
  the rest) and St. Nino's converts (Salome & Perozhavra → Mirian & Nana).
- **The Latin West** — the **Lérins school** (Honoratus OS-0468 + Vincent, Caesarius, Eucherius,
  Lupus, Veranus); Hilary of Poitiers; the Synod-of-Whitby circle (Cedd OS-0403).
- **Serbia** — **Sava of Serbia** OS-0449 (Nemanjić family) and the **Branković household**
  (OS-0482); **Mark of Ephesus** OS-0486 (Council of Florence).
- **Russian Northern Thebaid** — the Cyril-of-White-Lake circle (Martinian OS-0435), the
  Alexander-of-Svir disciples (Athanasius of Syandemsk OS-0477), and many Kiev-Caves monks
  (founder links to Anthony OS-0136 / Theodosius OS-2745).
- **Confessor cohort** — the 9th-c. iconoclasm confessors under Leo the Armenian (Emilian of
  Cyzicus OS-0406 + Nikephoros, Euthymius of Sardis, Michael of Synnada, Theophylact, Joseph).

## 4. Methodology & quality

Unchanged v1.3 pipeline (two 50-profile batches × five sub-range subagents → central
consolidation). This range stress-tested the **conflation guard** hardest yet — three Anthonys, two
Athanasii, four-plus Macarii, four Euthymii, two Irenarchi, multiple Theodosii/Gregorys/Cyrils — and
it held: agents disambiguated by century/region/epithet and left plain cards where identity was
uncertain. They also **corrected two of my cluster hints** (OS-0475 is Achilles the Anchorite, not
Achillius of Larissa; OS-0476 is Cyril of Alexandria) and **re-bucketed three anachronistic
"companion" cards** into `related`. Five new note-prefixes were added to the standards (`Translated`,
`Hagiographer`/`Hagiographer of`, `Monastic model`, `Later patron`, `Same city`).
