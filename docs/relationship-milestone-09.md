## 3. What was built this phase

OS-0801–0900 spans the patristic family hubs, the iconoclasm confessors, the Georgian and Slavic
monastic worlds, and the Celtic/Frankish/Italo-Greek West:

- **Family & patristic** — the Nazianzus family (Caesarius → Gregory the Theologian, Gorgonia,
  Nonna); Benedict of Nursia & Scholastica; the Smyrna martyr Pionius.
- **Iconoclasm confessors** — a dense ninth-century cohort (Theophanes of Sigriane, Niketas, James of
  Catania, Euschemon, Hilarion of Pelekete, Stephen of Triglia, Eustathius).
- **Georgia** — the Bagrationi kings (Demetrius II), the modern confessors (Kirion II, Ambrose), the
  Khakhuli–Iveron Athonite network (John of Khakhuli, Arsen of Ninotsminda).
- **Russia** — the Kazan martyrs and hierarchs, Macarius of Kalyazin, the Kiev Caves founders' circle,
  the Euphrosynus-of-Pskov disciples, Nikolai of Zhicha, and the Roslavl → Optina eldership.
- **The West** — Gertrude of Nivelles (Frankish), Enda of Aran (Irish), Vitalius (Italo-Greek), Cyril
  of Jerusalem, the Mar Saba martyrs.
- **Martyric cohorts** — the Martyrs-under-Julian (Mark of Arethusa, Cyril of Heliopolis), the Persian
  Shapur II martyrs (Jonah & Barachisius), the 26 Goths, the Caesarea-Palestine martyrs.

## 4. Database Expansion stream

**13 candidates** logged (register → **63**), heavy on the Western (Frankish/Italo-Greek/Welsh-adjacent)
and Jerusalem/early-Roman gaps. The phase produced the stream's **fourth false-negative correction**:
Arsen of Ninotsminda was flagged "absent" but exists as **OS-1618** — the candidate was dropped and
OS-0807 wired to the existing row. The grep-confirm-before-logging discipline is holding (it caught
the others), and the false-negative note now lists all four (Dionysius=OS-1964, Jerome=OS-1362,
Modestus=OS-0066, Arsen=OS-1618).

## 5. Methodology & quality

Unchanged v1.3 pipeline. Adversarial judgment continues to be the highest-value quality lever —
this phase corrected four cluster hints (Anton Meskhi ≠ Anton of Martqopi; Euthymius of Demitsana ≠
the Iberian; Thomas of Constantinople post-dates Gregory the Great; Artemon of Seleucia ≠ of
Laodicea) and self-corrected two anachronistic companion cards. The conflation guard held across two
Quadrati, three Sophronii, two Nikons, two Hilarions, and two Marks.
