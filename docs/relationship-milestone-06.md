## 3. What was built this phase

OS-0501–0600 is theologically and geographically broad — Byzantine theologians, Syrian and Sinai
ascetics, Georgian and Serbian saints, Russian northern monastics, and several Gospel/early-martyr
anchors:

- **Maximus the Confessor** OS-0503 — the 7th-c. Monothelite-confessor circle (Sophronius, Pope
  Martin, his disciple Anastasius) plus a theological-heir chain to Symeon the New Theologian and
  Gregory Palamas.
- **Syrian Solitaries** — a large cohort from Theodoret's *Historia Religiosa* (Marcian, Mausimas,
  Salamanes, Macedonius, Publius, Mares, Palladius, Aphraates, Zeno of Antioch, Peter of Galatia)
  cross-woven as "fellow Syrian solitary" links — a major new candidate cluster.
- **Holy Unmercenaries** — Cyrus & John OS-0568 wired with the Anargyroi template and the co-martyr
  Athanasia and her daughters.
- **Presentation of the Lord** — Simeon the God-Receiver & Anna the Prophetess anchored to Christ
  (OS-0000) and the Theotokos (OS-0001).
- **Russian** — Optina (Anatole I), Hierarchs of Perm, the Kiev-Caves cohort, and the three
  Hungarian brothers (Ephraim of Novy Torg → Moses the Hungarian → Boris & Gleb).
- **Georgian & Serbian** — the Bagrationi royal house (Ashot), the Chosroid line (Archil), the
  Serbian Peć succession (Jacob), and the Georgian Thirteen-Syrian-Fathers chain (Evagrius → Shio).
- **Early Church** — Hippolytus of Rome (Pontian/Callistus), the Caesarea–Palestine martyrs, the
  Cyprus circle of Epiphanius, and the Sicilian virgin-martyrs (Agatha ↔ Lucy).

## 4. Database Expansion stream (first full phase)

The new fourth stream is operational: subagents flag every unmatched plain card and classify it as a
genuine commemorated-saint candidate or an exclusion. **9 candidates** were logged this phase
(Eustochium, Marcella of Rome, Cybi of Holyhead, Meletios the Younger, Felix of Nola, Nektarios &
Theophanes of Meteora, Martyr Isidore, George the Hungarian), bringing the register to **25**.
Excluded with reasons: Theodoret of Cyrus (disputed canonization) and Gennadius Scholarius.

## 5. Methodology & quality

Unchanged v1.3 pipeline. The conflation guard again held across a dense same-name field (two Zenos,
two Xenophons, two Ephraims, two Anastasii, multiple Simeons/Cyrils/Nicholases), and agents kept the
Chosroid and Bagrationi Georgian dynasties distinct. Two of my cluster hints were corrected
(OS-0511 = Betania confessor, not George the Hagiorite; no Palladius/Simeon shared feast). The
Theodoret-of-Cyrus question recurred and was resolved conservatively (chronicler, never carded;
excluded from the expansion register as disputed).
